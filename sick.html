Sickle Cell Disease

Sickle cell disease (SCD) is an inherited blood disorder that affects red blood cells. The normal red blood cell contain mostly hemoglobin A but in SCD, hemoglobin* S (an abnormal type of hemoglobin) is also found in variable fraction along with HbA . Depending on the quantity of HbS, an individual is categorized as Sickle cell trait or disease. Usually trait may not have any manifestation but SCD does. In disease, the red blood cells become sickle-shaped (crescent shaped) and have difficulty in passing through small blood vessels. They either adhere to vessel wall or get stuck to each other and blocks small blood vessels. Tissue gets deprived of oxygenated blood as it does not receive a normal blood flow. This is how vaso-oclusive crisis occurs. It is the most common manifestation of the disease. After repeated tissue injury, eventually organ failure is the end result. Bone marrow transplantation is the only method of cure for sickle cell disease, but not feasible for general individual.

Sickling hemoglobinopathies are inherited disorders that result in production of an abnormal form of hemoglobin. Beta thalassemias are inherited disorders that result in the decreased synthesis or complete absence of the beta globin chains of hemoglobin. Sickle cell beta thalassemia (Hb S/β Th) is an inherited form of sickle cell disease that affects red blood cells both in the production of abnormal hemoglobin, as well as the decreased synthesis of beta globin chains. Individuals with sickle cell beta thalassemia have one abnormal beta chain, βS, and a defective beta-globin gene, either in decreased synthesis, β+, or complete absence of synthesis, β0.

Red blood cells with HbS have shorter half life than normal red cells i.e. 20 days Vs 120 days. They break down ( hemolyse) and cause anemia and jaundice.

Disease and its Inheritance : The abnormal hemoglobin from the carrier parents ( carrying one defective gene) are transmitted to their offspring. If one defective gene is inherited, then it is a carrier (50% chances) and if two defective genes from both parents are transmitted , then it will manifest with disease ( 25% chances). But there is also 25% chances to inherit normal genes from both parents, then it is neither a carrier nor with disease. Through genetic awareness, pre-marital counseling and Anti-natal molecular diagnosis, our goal is to bring up generations free from disease.

A simple blood test known as HPLC or Hemoglobin Electrophoresis will analyze the composition of hemoglobin and determine the type of hemoglobin disorder, whether Beta-2 thalassemia, Sickle Beta thaassmia or Sickle cell disease.

Sickle cells are destroyed rapidly, thus causing anemia, jaundice or formation of gallstones. Pain episodes due to vessel blockage may cause acute chest syndrome, stroke and priapism . It may also causes damage to vital organs including the spleen, kidney, brain, bone and liver. Patients are prone to bacterial infections, as the spleen function gets compromised with increasing age.

Treatment of complications often includes antibiotics, pain management, intravenous fluids, blood transfusion and many at times needs comprehensive multi-disciplinary care. Life style modification and psychosocial support plays an important role.

Therefore, from early age, the sickle cell disease needs to be managedstarts with early diagnosis, preferably in the newborn period and includes penicillin prophylaxis, vaccination against pneumococcus bacteria and folic acid supplementation.
