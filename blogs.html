Stories of success :

Case I:

16 yrs Hemophilia A boy, slipped and had trivial injury on his shoulder and head. His head injury went un-noticed by the patient but he had swelling in Right shoulder. He had high titre of inhibitor, that is the reason why he couldnot have specific treatment. Read more None could recollect about head injury. He reported in private Hospital at emergency in disorientation state( post 4 days of injury) and within 6 hours he started uttering repeated monosyllable and finally had aphasia. The CT Scan of brain revealed brain hemorrhage ( intracranial bleed with Subdural hematoma). On emergency, neurosurgeon decided to reduce the mass effect on brain by decompression osteotomy with drainage. It was a challanging state to plan for operation in the absence of recombinant Factor VII. In the joint effort of STHM and company support, worth of 5 lakhs expenditure, the 1st operation was done in one sitting. The parital scalp bone was removed and kept in abdominal fat for 2 months. After stabilization, again 2nd sitting of operation was done to replace back the scalp bone by removing from abdomen.

Case IV :

PWH (person with Hemophilia A ) of 23 yrs male, had hematoma in psoas muscle, which was reported as psoas abscess. Surgery was planned without getting into work up for bleeding profile. On the OT table, the abdomen was opened to drain the abscess. Read more But the situation lend into intractable bleed. On emergency basis, the wound was packed with guaze and he was sent to tertiary care hospital for further management. He was diagnosed with FACTOR VIII deficiency. On past history, he used to have often bleed event in the knee which was considered as rheumatoid arthritis and was treated for pain management. Now the approach was focussed on infusion of factor concentrate and was kept on ICU with nil orally for 15 days with the hope for conservative mode of healing. The modality of treatment was changed with us. First the patient was counseled for reducing his apprehension and anxiety of fear of bleeding. Then he was made to take food orally. Under the coverage of factor concentrates, the VAC was applied and he was made mobile from the next day. It was applied for 5 days. The deep cut wound was closed from within and finally stitched. On twelth day, his stitch was opened and he got discharged on 14th day.

Case VI :

A 17 yrs PWH had a post traumatic non-healing ulcerated knee lesion and that was gradually worsening with increase in dimension. He was wandering from hospital to hospital during the COVID time for 2 months. Read more Every time he was tested for COVID and kept on admission for factor infusion, antibiotics and half hearted dressing of the wound at the government hospital with factor supply. Basic mistake was with the non-availability of diagnostic support like inhibitor screening etc. The PWH reported to us, when he was asked to go outside state for treatment. With proper dressing , a streamlined procedure of treatment with judicious use of factor concentrate, he got completely cured from non-healing ulcer of the knee within 14 days and was discharged from hospital.

Solution :

Factor concentrates are made available in many parts of the country. Hemophilia federation of india, State Government and various hemophilia societies work for PWH to make India pain and disability free. Overall system will take time to reach the level of expectation in a vast country like india. But necessity of the PWH can make them to learn the facts faster. The mission of disability free india can only be successful with implementation of self infusion, home therapy and judicious use of factor. To educate PWH and those people who deal with this kind of disease need to understand the bleeding problem along with the social issues. Rather than providing excuses of no staff or withdrawl of health system to ignore these patients due to increased work burden, all district head quarter hospital should volunteer to procure factor concentrates ( provided by govt.) and enlist themselves as service providers for hemophilia, if a hemophilia patient is present in their district.

Conclusion :

There are innumerable stories. The more we understand the disease and its complications, less mistake will be committed. Factor concentrates are very costly resource. Govt. sector and Hemophilia federation of India (HFI) through local hemophilia societies provide factor concentrates, free of cost through out the country. World Federation of Hemophilia (WFH)has remarkable contribution for PWH. It supports HFI with factor concentrates and has provided guidelines for management of PWH. Our responsibility is to follow them and make the best possible utilization of resources.
