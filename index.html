Mission, Goal & Vision:

Overview

Sickle cell, Thalassemia and Hemophilia Mission (STHM) is an organization for under-priviledged, ignorant group of patients with sickle cell, thalassemia and hemophilia. It extends a co-ordinated help from Physicians of different field and mediates general Public help to these section of people. It is founded as a non-profit, non-governmental organization, registered in the year 2018, Reg. no. 41131808632 ; Dt. 19-11-18

It was started with an intent to promote awareness about inheritance ( disease transmission), treatment (as per the guidelines) and prevention of the above three genetic blood disorder.

How STHM functions?

At ground level:

We formulate the network with District headquarter hospitals, blood bank officers, local stakeholders, NGOs, to pursue diagnosis, treatment for existing patients and spread the message of prevention at different platforms. It got started with commitment to increase the awareness about the disease and its treatment. Through district visit, we reach every common individual to participate in the mission in any form, either as blood donor or in promotion of prevention. Our effort is : To restore normalcy in the life of patients living with Thalassemia, sickle cell disease and Hemophilia regardless of their financial status by enabling access to improved care within limited resources. To sensitize public about the diseases and encourage voluntary blood donations. To boost the knowledge of "right time to act", answer the ignorance by providing enough information about disease, management, cure and its prevention.

At Hospital level (SUM Hospital, Bhubaneswar):

Deliver OPD consultation, Genetic counseling. Provide Leuco-depleted fresh packed red blood cell Antigen negative fresh packed red cell for alloimmunized cases Extended red cell phenotyping Facilitates blood transfusion at Day care as per the guidelines Comprehensive Health care service for complicated cases. Networking and co-ordination for prenatal diagnosis within fetal medicine and genetic lab.

At Mega Camps:

Blood Investigation : CBC, biochemical test like ferritin, Liver function test, screening for Transfusion transmitted infection, sample collection for HLA testing, Mutational analysis for prevention, screening of fit candidates for bone marrow transplantation. Counseling sessions and free check up and consultation. Blood donation drive and Organize Blood donation camp.

STHM Activity domains:

Genetic Awareness camps Motivational blood donation campaign Camp of Investigation and treatment support Facilitates Hospital based speciality care for STH group of patients Adds direction and networking to prevention through genetic counseling and pre-natal diagnosis Blood bank support for allo-immunized cases Educate the patient to stand for their own needs In Govt. health sector of various districts, laisoning with the officials for essential requirements at their respective hospitals. Extend clinical support to other thalassemia societies.

Goal

To reduce the social burden of these genetical inherited blood disorder through awareness of inheritance, intensive screening and implementation of prevention program. Encourage social participation for successful prevention program.

Mission

To improve the quality of life Make every patient learn the tact of living with disease Prevent complications by enlightening them with knowledge of disease management ( Self care ).

Vision

Disability free

Pain free

Free from social trauma and fear

Thalassemia:

A condition of blood deficiency from early childhood. They have to undergo life long repeated blood transfusion along with other medication to remove iron from their body.

If not sufficiently transfused, develop thalassemic facies, growth retardation, hepatosplenomegaly. Learn More

Haemophilia:

The patients are mostly male. A condition in which they are deficient of clotting factor which promote bleed slowly over long time. Joints are common to be affected in bleed events. They need to get infused with deficient factors or blood products immediately. If not treated timely or promptly, suffer from pain, disability, joint impairment for long time. The adjacent muscles become weak and thinned out. Then they need support with walker, crutch, knee brace etc. Learn More
